Biography

Education

Audrey Hepburn, the iconic actress and humanitarian, had a unique and challenging upbringing. Born on May 4, 1929, in Brussels, Belgium, as Audrey Kathleen Ruston, she experienced the impact of World War II during her formative years.
Early Life and War Years: Audrey's parents were Dutch baroness Ella van Heemstra and English banker Joseph Hepburn-Ruston. Her parents divorced when she was six, and Audrey moved to the Netherlands with her mother. During World War II, the family faced hardships due to the German occupation. Audrey and her family suffered from food shortages and witnessed the hardships of war.
Ballet Training: Audrey had a passion for dance from a young age and began studying ballet in Arnhem, Netherlands. Her dream was to become a professional ballet dancer. However, her aspirations were hindered by the effects of malnutrition during the war, which stunted her growth.
Early Modeling and Acting: Audrey's path to fame began when she worked as a model in London, where she was discovered by a producer. She made her film debut in the British film "One Wild Oat" in 1941. She continued to pursue acting and moved to Hollywood in 1953, where she gained recognition for her role in "Roman Holiday," winning the Academy Award for Best Actress.
Continued Education and Learning: Despite her success in acting, Audrey Hepburn valued education. She continued to educate herself throughout her life, expressing an interest in learning and reading. Her experiences during the war likely contributed to her appreciation for the value of education.
Humanitarian Work: Audrey Hepburn's life was not only defined by her acting career but also by her commitment to humanitarian causes. She served as a UNICEF Goodwill Ambassador and worked tirelessly on behalf of children in need around the world. Her experiences during World War II, where she witnessed the suffering of children, influenced her dedication to humanitarian work.
Audrey Hepburn's education was shaped not only by formal schooling and artistic training but also by her life experiences, including the challenges of war and her passion for helping others. Her legacy extends beyond her contributions to film, as she remains an enduring symbol of elegance, compassion, and philanthropy.

Career

Audrey Hepburn's career was marked by her extraordinary talent, grace, and timeless elegance. She achieved success in various aspects of the entertainment industry, including acting, modeling, and humanitarian work. Here's an overview of Audrey Hepburn's notable career milestones:
Early Modeling Career: Audrey started her career as a ballet dancer and model in London during the post-war years. Her unique beauty and style caught the attention of photographers and designers, leading to her work as a successful model. She appeared in numerous magazines and advertisements.
Breakthrough in Hollywood: Audrey's breakthrough in Hollywood came with her role as Princess Ann in the 1953 film "Roman Holiday." Her performance was widely acclaimed, earning her the Academy Award for Best Actress. This success catapulted her to stardom and established her as a leading actress in Hollywood.
Iconic Film Roles: Audrey Hepburn went on to star in a series of iconic films, solidifying her status as a Hollywood legend. Some of her most memorable roles include:"Sabrina" (1954), "Breakfast at Tiffany's" (1961), "My Fair Lady" (1964), "Charade" (1963), "Funny Face" (1957) and "The Nun's Story" (1959)
Audrey Hepburn took a hiatus from acting in the late 1960s to focus on her family. She returned to the screen in the 1970s and 1980s with films like "Robin and Marian" (1976) and "They All Laughed" (1981). Although her later film career was not as prolific as her earlier years, she continued to be a beloved figure.
In the latter part of her life, Audrey dedicated a significant amount of time to humanitarian causes. As a UNICEF Goodwill Ambassador, she worked on behalf of children in need around the world. Her commitment to humanitarian efforts became a defining aspect of her legacy.
She is remembered not only for her exceptional talent on screen but also for her grace, kindness, and philanthropy. Her timeless style and contributions to both the entertainment industry and humanitarian causes have left an enduring legacy.

Family

Parents: Ella van Heemstra: Audrey's mother was a Dutch baroness descended from minor European nobility. She supported Audrey during her early years in the Netherlands and shared a close bond with her. Joseph Victor Anthony Ruston: Audrey's father was an English banker. Her parents divorced when she was six years old, and Audrey moved with her mother to the Netherlands.
Siblings: Ian Quarles van Ufford: Audrey's half-brother from her mother's first marriage. He played a role in Audrey's life, and they maintained a close relationship.
Marriages: Mel Ferrer: Audrey's first husband was the American actor and producer Mel Ferrer. They were married in 1954 and had one son together, Sean Hepburn Ferrer, born in 1960. The couple divorced in 1968 after facing marital difficulties. Andrea Dotti: Audrey's second husband was the Italian psychiatrist Andrea Dotti. They were married in 1969 and had a son named Luca Dotti, born in 1970. This marriage also ended in divorce in 1982.
Children: Sean Hepburn Ferrer: Audrey's son with Mel Ferrer. He has been involved in various projects related to his mother's legacy, including the documentary "Remembering Audrey." Luca Dotti: Audrey's son with Andrea Dotti. He has co-authored books about his mother, providing insights into her life and sharing family photographs.
Later Generations: Audrey Hepburn's grandchildren include Sean Ferrer's children and Luca Dotti's children. They have, at times, continued to honor and preserve their grandmother's legacy.

Awards

  • Academy Awards (Oscars): 1954: Academy Award for Best Actress for her role in "Roman Holiday."
  • Golden Globe Awards: 1954: Golden Globe Award for Best Actress in a Motion Picture – Drama for "Roman Holiday." 1956: Henrietta Award (World Film Favorite – Female) 1957: Golden Globe Award for Best Actress in a Motion Picture – Musical or Comedy for "Sabrina."
  • BAFTA Awards: 1954: British Academy Film Award for Best British Actress for "Roman Holiday." 1955: BAFTA Award for Best Actress in a Leading Role for "Sabrina."
  • Tony Awards: 1954: Special Tony Award (along with the cast) for her performance in the Broadway play "Ondine."
  • Emmy Awards: 1993: Posthumously awarded the Primetime Emmy Award for Outstanding Individual Achievement in Informational Programming for her documentary "Gardens of the World with Audrey Hepburn."
  • Screen Actors Guild Awards: 1993: Posthumously awarded the Screen Actors Guild Life Achievement Award.
  • Cecil B. DeMille Award: 1990: Honored with the Cecil B. DeMille Award at the Golden Globe Awards for outstanding contributions to the world of entertainment.
  • Other Honors: Presidential Medal of Freedom: In 1992, Audrey Hepburn was posthumously awarded the Presidential Medal of Freedom by President George H. W. Bush for her humanitarian work.
